MRI:

MRI, also known as Magnetic Resonance Imaging, uses NMR. MRI scans use NMR to produce images of body structures. MRI is an application of NMR, which could be used to generate multi-dimensional images.

Analyzing Organic Compounds:

NMR Spectroscopy is one of the most widely used methods to analyze organic substances. It can take both solid and liquid samples, and measure the chemical shift. This allows for easy identification of substances and chemical mapping of the sample.

Food Quality Control:

NMR can be used to map protein structures and identify any additives. It can analyze the chemical composition of foods and help maintain the food quality standard.
